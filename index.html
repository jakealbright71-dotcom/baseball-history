Origins of Baseball

Baseball evolved from older bat-and-ball games like rounders and cricket in the mid-19th century. While Abner Doubleday was once credited, historians now recognize Alexander Cartwright as a key figure in establishing modern rules.

Key Moments in U.S. Baseball

  • 1845 - Knickerbocker Rules established in New York
  • 1869 - First professional team: Cincinnati Red Stockings
  • 1903 - First World Series: Boston vs Pittsburgh
  • 1947 - Jackie Robinson breaks color barrier
  • 1973 - Designated hitter rule introduced

Baseball Vocabulary

Diamond

The four bases arranged in a square shape that players run around.

Inning

A period of play where both teams have a turn at batting.

Home Run

When a batter hits the ball and circles all bases to score.

Pitcher

The player who throws the baseball toward the batter.
